GOAL 12:
RESPONSIBLE CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION


"Responsible Consumption and Production," encapsulated within Goal 12 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, aims to transform patterns of consumption and production towards sustainability. This goal recognizes the urgent need to reduce the environmental impact of human activities and promote more responsible and efficient resource use.
Goal 12 emphasizes the importance of minimizing waste generation, promoting recycling and reuse, and adopting sustainable production practices. This involves reducing overconsumption, adopting eco-friendly technologies, and ensuring that the lifecycle of products has minimal negative impact on the environment. Efforts towards responsible consumption and production include raising awareness about the environmental impact of consumer choices and encouraging sustainable behaviors. This may involve promoting sustainable consumption patterns, such as buying locally-produced goods, choosing products with minimal packaging, and supporting businesses that prioritize environmental and social responsibility. Inefficient production methods, excessive resource use, and improper waste management contribute to environmental degradation and depletion of natural resources. Achieving Goal 12 requires collaborative efforts between governments, businesses, and individuals to implement sustainable practices. Businesses are encouraged to adopt eco-friendly production processes and incorporate circular economy principles that reduce waste and extend the life of products.
While progress has been made in raising awareness and adopting more sustainable practices, challenges such as the global growth in consumption and the rising demand for resources persist. Sustainable development requires a shift from a throwaway culture to one that values resource efficiency, innovation, and conscious choices. By fostering responsible consumption and production, societies can move closer to achieving environmental sustainability while improving the well-being of present and future generations.

THE TARGETS

Everyone can help to make sure that we meet the Global Goals. Use these twelve targets to create action for responsible consumption and production.

  • HALVE GLOBAL PER CAPITA FOOD WASTE
    By 2030, halve per capita global food waste at the retail and consumer levels and reduce food losses along production and supply chains, including post-harvest losses.
  • REMOVE MARKET DISTORTIONS THAT ENCOURAGE WASTEFUL CONSUMPTION
    Rationalize inefficient fossil-fuel subsidies that encourage wasteful consumption by removing market distortions, in accordance with national circumstances, including by restructuring taxation and phasing out those harmful subsidies, where they exist, to reflect their environmental impacts, taking fully into account the specific needs and conditions of developing countries and minimizing the possible adverse impacts on their development in a manner that protects the poor and the affected communities.
  • PROMOTE UNIVERSAL UNDERSTANDING OF SUSTAINABLE LIFESTYLES
    By 2030, ensure that people everywhere have the relevant information and awareness for sustainable development and lifestyles in harmony with nature.
  • RESPONSIBLE MANAGEMENT OF CHEMICALS AND WASTE
    By 2020, achieve the environmentally sound management of chemicals and all wastes throughout their life cycle, in accordance with agreed international frameworks, and significantly reduce their release to air, water and soil in order to minimize their adverse impacts on human health and the environment.
  • SUSTAINABLE MANAGEMENT AND USE OF NATURAL RESOURCES By 2030, achieve the sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources.
