GOAL 5:
GENDER EQUALITY


Gender equality is a cornerstone of modern human rights and a crucial component of social and economic progress. It encompasses a vision where all individuals, regardless of their gender, have the same rights, opportunities, and capacities to participate fully in all aspects of life. This fundamental principle is articulated in Goal 5 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which seeks to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls by 2030.
At the heart of the gender equality goal is the aspiration to eliminate deeply rooted gender-based discrimination, biases, and inequalities that persist in societies worldwide. This goes beyond merely addressing women's rights; it involves redefining cultural norms, rols, and expectations that historically relegated women to subordinate positions. Achieving gender equality requires not only addressing direct forms of discrimination but also challenging the more subtle, systemic, and often unconscious biases that perpetuate inequalities. A key component of gender equality is ensuring equal access to education for all genders. Historically, girls and women have faced barriers to education, limiting their opportunities for personal growth and economic empowerment. By ensuring that girls have the same access to quality education as boys, societies can unlock the potential of half their population, driving economic growth, reducing poverty, and promoting sustainable development.
Economic empowerment is another vital aspect of gender equality. Ensuring that women have equal access to economic resources, opportunities, and decision-making power contributes not only to their individual well-being but also to the overall prosperity of communities and nations. When women are able to participate fully in the workforce and have control over financial resources, they can invest in their families' health, education, and future prospects, creating a positive ripple effect for generations to come. Gender equality is also closely linked to health and well-being. Addressing gender-specific health issues, such as maternal mortality and reproductive health, is crucial for ensuring the well-being of women and girls. It also involves challenging harmful practices like child marriage and female genital mutilation, which violate the rights and dignity of girls and women.

THE TARGETS

Everyone can help to make sure that we meet the Global Goals. Use these nine targets to create action for gender equality.

  • END DISCRIMINATION AGAINST WOMEN AND GIRLS
    End all forms of discrimination against all women and girls everywhere.
  • ELIMINATE FORCED MARRIAGES AND GENITAL MUTILATION
    Eliminate all harmful practices, such as child, early and forced marriage and female genital mutilation.
  • ADOPT AND STRENGTHEN POLICIES AND ENFORCEABLE LEGISLATION FOR GENDER EQUALITY
    Adopt and strengthen sound policies and enforceable legislation for the promotion of gender equality and the empowerment of all women and girls at all levels.
  • END ALL VIOLENCE AGAINST AND EXPLOITATION OF WOMEN AND GIRLS
    Eliminate all forms of violence against all women and girls in the public and private spheres, including trafficking and sexual and other types of exploitation.
  • VALUE UNPAID CARE AND PROMOTE SHARED DOMESTIC RESPONSIBILITIES
    Recognize and value unpaid care and domestic work through the provision of public services, infrastructure and social protection policies and the promotion of shared responsibility within the household and the family as nationally appropriate.
  • UNIVERSAL ACCESS TO REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH AND RIGHTS
    Ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health and reproductive rights as agreed in accordance with the Programme of Action of the International Conference on Population and Development and the Beijing Platform for Action and the outcome documents of their review conferences.
  • PROMOTE EMPOWERMENT OF WOMEN THROUGH TECHNOLOGY
    Enhance the use of enabling technology, in particular information and communications technology, to promote the empowerment of women.
