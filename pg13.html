GOAL 13:
CLIMATE ACTION

53%


"Climate Action," represented by Goal 13 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, underscores the urgent need to address climate change and its impacts. This goal recognizes that human activities have led to rising global temperatures, severe weather events, and environmental degradation, threatening ecosystems and livelihoods worldwide.
Goal 13 emphasizes the importance of taking concrete steps to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, enhance resilience to climate impacts, and promote sustainable practices. This involves transitioning to clean and renewable energy sources, increasing energy efficiency, and adopting policies that limit carbon emissions. Efforts toward climate action include international agreements like the Paris Agreement, which aim to limit global warming and guide nations in their efforts to mitigate and adapt to climate change. Additionally, adaptation strategies are crucial for communities to cope with the changing climate and minimize its negative effects, particularly on vulnerable populations. Mitigating climate change requires the collaboration of governments, businesses, civil society, and individuals. Policies that incentivize sustainable practices, investments in clean technologies, and public awareness campaigns can drive meaningful change. By curbing emissions, protecting ecosystems, and fostering sustainable development, societies can mitigate the worst impacts of climate change and create a more sustainable future for all.
While progress has been made, the challenge of climate change remains significant. Urgent and transformative actions are needed to limit global warming and safeguard the planet's ecosystems and biodiversity. Goal 13 is a rallying point for global cooperation and action, reminding us that by taking steps to address climate change, we are securing a healthier and more sustainable future for generations to come.

THE TARGETS

Everyone can help to make sure that we meet the Global Goals. Use these five targets to create action to combat climate change.

  • STRENGTHEN RESILIENCE AND ADAPTIVE CAPACITY TO CLIMATE RELATED DISASTERS
    Strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-related hazards and natural disasters in all countries.
  • BUILD KNOWLEDGE AND CAPACITY TO MEET CLIMATE CHANGE
    Improve education, awareness-raising and human and institutional capacity on climate change mitigation, adaptation, impact reduction and early warning.
  • PROMOTE MECHANISMS TO RAISE CAPACITY FOR PLANNING AND MANAGEMENT
    Promote mechanisms for raising capacity for effective climate change-related planning and management in least developed countries and small island developing States, including focusing on women, youth and local and marginalized communities.
  • IMPLEMENT THE UN FRAMEWORK CONVENTION ON CLIMATE CHANGE
    Implement the commitment undertaken by developed-country parties to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change to a goal of mobilizing jointly $100 billion annually by 2020 from all sources to address the needs of developing countries in the context of meaningful mitigation actions and transparency on implementation and fully operationalize the Green Climate Fund through its capitalization as soon as possible.
  • INTEGRATE CLIMATE CHANGE MEASURES INTO POLICIES AND PLANNING
    Integrate climate change measures into national policies, strategies and planning.
